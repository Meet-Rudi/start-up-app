# MEET_RUDI — Consent & Privacy Notice

## How to read this document

This document has two parts:

1. **Part A — The consent you give** (short, plain, the thing you tick/confirm).
2. **Part B — The full privacy notice** (the detail behind Part A, so your consent
   is *informed* as GDPR requires).

You should read Part A before you agree, and Part B whenever you want the full
picture. Nothing about your health is processed until you have given the consent
in Part A.

# Part A — Your consent

**Who you're talking to.** "Rudi" is an automated conversation companion delivered
over WhatsApp by **Meet Rudi BV** ("MeetRudi", "we", "us"), in cooperation with
**[PILOT ORGANISATION NAME]**, the organisation through which you were invited to
take part. Rudi chats with you about your everyday lifestyle — movement, food,
daily activity and related habits — to help you stick to the plan your own
clinician or care team has already set for you.

**What Rudi is *not*.** Rudi is **not a medical device**, does **not diagnose or
treat** anything, and does **not give medical advice**. It supports a plan someone
else prescribed. For anything clinical — symptoms, medication decisions,
emergencies — you must contact your own doctor or the emergency services. If you
tell Rudi about a possible health risk, it will gently steer you back to your care
team rather than advise you itself.

**What you are agreeing to.** By confirming below, you give your **explicit
consent** for us to:

- receive and read the WhatsApp messages you send Rudi — **text, photos (for
  example, of your meals), and voice messages** — and reply to them;
- process information about your **health and lifestyle** that you share this way
  (this is "special category" data under GDPR Article 9, which is why we ask you so
  explicitly);
- **remember your case over time** — keep a profile and a memory of your past
  conversations so Rudi stays relevant and doesn't make you repeat yourself;
- **message you first, about 2–3 times a week**, when you've gone quiet, as a light
  "tap on the shoulder" nudge to continue the conversation.

**This is your choice, and you can change your mind.** Taking part is voluntary.
You can **withdraw your consent at any time** — just tell Rudi "stop" (or contact
us using Part B) — and we will stop processing and, on request, delete your data.
Withdrawing is as easy as giving consent, it costs you nothing, and it does not
affect the care you receive from your clinician or from [PILOT ORGANISATION NAME].
Withdrawal does not undo processing we already, lawfully did before you withdrew.

**Confirm your consent:**

      B), and I **explicitly consent** to the Rudi service processing my **health
      and lifestyle information** — the text, food photos and voice messages I send

*One confirmation covers the whole Rudi service described above. You can withdraw it

### 2. What data we process

- **Messages you send Rudi:** text, photos (e.g. of meals), and voice/audio
  messages.
- **Information within those messages** about your movement, diet, daily activity,
  habits, how you're feeling, and your progress against your plan — i.e. **health
  and lifestyle data (Article 9 special category)**.
- **Your profile and memory:** goals, constraints, your care plan as relevant to
  lifestyle, your language, your consent record, and a running summary and
  long-term memory derived from your conversations.
- **Technical metadata** needed to run the service: your WhatsApp identifier, which
  Rudi persona/number you're paired with, and message timestamps and delivery
  status.

We deliberately keep this to what we need — we don't ask for data we don't use.

### 3. Why we process it and our legal basis

| Purpose | Legal basis (GDPR) |
|---|---|
| Running the Rudi service for you: processing your **health & lifestyle** information (messages, food photos, voice) **and remembering your case over time** (profile, summaries, long-term memory) to support adherence to your care plan | **Explicit consent** — Art. 6(1)(a) **and** Art. 9(2)(a) |
| Sending proactive check-in messages (~2–3×/week) | Explicit consent — Art. 6(1)(a) |
| Keeping records to prove consent, and safety/quality checks on the service | Our **legitimate interest** in running a safe, accountable service — Art. 6(1)(f) |

We rely on **your consent** for the health-related processing. That's why Part A
asks you so specifically, and why you can take it back at any time.

### 4. How Rudi works, and automated processing

Rudi's replies are generated automatically by AI. Before any message is sent to
you, it passes through a **safety gate** that checks for risk signals (for example,
signs of very low blood sugar or other red-flag content) and suppresses anything
that looks like medical advice, steering you to your clinician instead. **Sampled
conversations are reviewed by trained staff** for safety and quality (see §7).

This automated conversation does **not** make legal or similarly significant
decisions about you, and it never replaces your clinician's judgement. If you'd
ever like a human to look at your interaction, contact us using §11.

### 5. Where your data is processed and stored

- Your identifiable data — messages, profile, memory — is processed and stored
  **within the European Union**, on infrastructure hosted in the EU (Amazon Web
  Services, EU region).
- Some heavy AI computation runs on specialised EU-based GPU infrastructure. Before
  anything is sent there, it is **pseudonymised** — stripped of your name and
  contact details and replaced with an opaque ID — so that plane never receives
  data that directly identifies you.
- We do **not** transfer your personal data outside the EU/EEA for this service. If
  that ever needed to change, we would only do so under GDPR-approved safeguards and
  would tell you first.

### 6. Who else is involved (processors / sub-processors)

We use a small number of carefully chosen service providers, each bound by a **data
processing agreement** and each EU-region, in these roles:

- **WhatsApp messaging provider** — to deliver messages to and from you.
- **AI service providers** — for parts of the language, voice and image
  understanding.
- **Cloud & GPU hosting** — EU-region infrastructure that stores your data and runs
  the AI computation.

The specific companies acting in each of these roles are named, and kept current,
in our list of collaborating partners at
**https://meetrudi.eu/collaborating_partners.html**.

Note that WhatsApp itself is operated by Meta; your use of WhatsApp is also subject
to Meta's own terms and privacy policy, which we don't control.

### 7. Human review

To keep Rudi safe and accurate, trained members of our team review a **sample** of
conversations. Reviewers work under confidentiality obligations and only for
safety- and quality-improvement purposes. This is not clinical supervision of your
care.

### 8. How long we keep your data

- We keep your profile, memory and messages for **as long as you take part** in the
  service.
- If you **withdraw or ask us to delete**, we erase your data as described in §9.
- After you leave, we keep only the **minimum record needed to prove that valid
  consent existed** and to meet legal obligations, for **up to 24 months**, then
  delete it.

### 9. Your rights, including erasure

Under GDPR you can, at any time:

- **Access** the data we hold about you and get a copy;
- **Correct** anything inaccurate;
- **Erase** your data ("right to be forgotten");
- **Withdraw consent** — Rudi will stop, and processing based on that consent ends;
- **Restrict** or **object** to certain processing;
- **Port** your data to yourself or another provider in a machine-readable form;
- **Ask for human involvement** in the automated interaction (see §4).

When you ask us to erase your data, we delete it **across every store** — your
profile, the message/event log, the derived memory and vector recall, and cached
summaries — not just the most visible copy. Backups age out on their normal cycle
and are not restored to bring your data back.

To exercise any right, contact us at **privacy@meetrudi.be**, or simply
tell Rudi. We respond within one month.

### 10. If you're not happy

You can complain to your local data protection authority. In Belgium this is the
**Belgian Data Protection Authority (Gegevensbeschermingsautoriteit / Autorité de
protection des données)**, www.dataprotectionauthority.be. If you live elsewhere in
the EU, you can contact your national authority. We'd appreciate the chance to put
things right first — please reach out to us.
